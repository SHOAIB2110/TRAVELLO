Howrah Bridge

Howrah Bridge is a bridge with a suspended span over the Hooghly River in West Bengal, India. Commissioned in 1943, the bridge was originally named the New Howrah Bridge, because it replaced a pontoon bridge at the same location linking the two cities of Howrah and Kolkata. Wikipedia Total length: 705 m Construction started: 1935 Opened: 3 February 1943 Body of water: Hooghly River Location: Howrah, Kolkata, West Bengal Bridge type: Suspension bridge, Cantilever bridge, Pontoon bridge, Truss arch bridge
